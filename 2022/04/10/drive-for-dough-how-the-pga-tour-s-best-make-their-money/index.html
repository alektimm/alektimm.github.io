Drive for dough? How the PGA TOUR's best make their money

2022-04-10

Professional golf is undergoing a revolution in the way the game is played. Stronger players and significant advances in driver technology have led to a driving distance boom.

Spearheaded by the likes of Tiger Woods, John Daly, Rory McIlroy, Bryson Dechambeau, and numerous other bombers, PGA TOUR professionals are ditching the classic golf adage “drive for show, putt for dough.”

Professional golfers are terrific putters; however, it’s the driver that makes the money for the best in the world.

The players have caught on to this idea.

The average distance off the tee across all players on TOUR has climbed to 297 yards, nearly 30 yards more than Tiger Woods’ first full season in 1997. On a par 72 course with 4 par 3s, that is an extra 420 yards of distance added over that span.

The ability to separate yourself from the field with the driver is a major component of the best players in the world of late.

To find the best players, we can look at the leaders in PGA TOUR’s FedEx Cup Points standings across 2019-2021. Points are awarded for tournament finishes with the season-ending leader taking home the dough. $15 million to be exact.

Over that span, Jon Rahm, Justin Thomas, Patrick Cantlay, Dustin Johnson, and Tony Finau lead the way in FedEx Cup Points.

The key to each player’s success? Driving. The strokes gained statistics show it.

The PGA TOUR measures strokes gained, a calculation of how much better a player performs against their competitors. The calculation is based on recorded data of how many shots on average it takes players to hole the ball from all distances and situations.

If the field averages a score of 70 strokes and the player shoots 65, his total strokes gained would be +5. Thus, the higher the better.

Strokes gained can be broken down into different measurements for each aspect of the game—driving, approach shots, chipping, and putting. For example, if a player drives the ball to a more favorable location than the field average, they get a boost in their strokes gained: driving.

When it comes to the best players, they are gaining the most strokes from the field with the driver.
